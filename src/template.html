GOVERNMENT

How are laws passed in your colony?
Laws on the colony will largely be pre determined on Earth before the mission, but any laws that will need to be passed will be passed by either a full consensus or a vast supermajority of 90% or more. Because there are so few people on mars and the cooperation of the entire crew is so vital to sustaining the colony, a consensus is ideal as it means that everyone will be in agreement with one another. Additionally, because there are so little people, the drawbacks of consensus such as ineffectiveness in large numbers will not apply. However, the drawbacks to this system are that in moments where instantaneous decisions must be made, this approach is much too slow. As such, there will also be a mission commander that will be in charge of all operations and activities of the crew. Consensus will only be required for making laws.

How have you organized the executive, legislative and judicial branches?
The executive branch will simply consist of the entire crew. Each crew member will have their own assigned responsibilities and duties, such as maintaining water supply or doing surface maintenance EVA’s. As such, they will each function as a sort of “cabinet minister”, and together they will make up the “cabinet”. The mission commander will serve as either a prime minister or a dictator, with authority over the processes that take place. Similarly, the legislative branch will also consist of just crew members. Each person will serve as a Member of Parliament, and every person will participate in the “House of Commons” to make and revise laws. Because the ability to have your voice heard in such a small group is so important, punishments during the mission may involve being banned from participating in these sessions. However, because the judicial system may be dealing with crew members who have violated some sort of conduct, it will operate a bit differently than the other two branches. When deciding judgement for crew members that have broken the law, the crew member in question will of course not be permitted to take part in the decision. However, they will be allowed to state and argue their case. All other members will function as judges, deciding whether or not the person is guilty or not and what punishments to give. If no agreement is reached for any of the branches or under other circumstances, the mission commander has the power to override any other crew member’s decisions and make the decision themselves. This system will result in high cooperation rates and very effective communication, as all crew members are very active in the governmentation of the colony and play multiple roles in its different branches. When making laws, easy and consistent agreements will likely be reached and carrying out the laws is easy to oversee and regulate due to how small the government of the colony is. However, the mission commander’s power may result in them abusing their authority and establishing a dictatorship, and corruption amongst the crew is also likely. This governmentation system relies heavily on the crew member’s own moral values and the ability for the crew to make responsible and mature decisions on their own. For this reason, the system will not work with too many people or without rigorous screening when selecting crew members.

How does your colony choose members of the legislative branch?
The “prime Minister” of the colony will be predetermined on Earth, and a rank of power will also be decided. Any other crew members will automatically be selected as Members of Parliament, should they wish to contribute in the making and revising of laws. This way, all crew members will be able to participate in law making and no one will feel that their opinion is left out. However, as the number of people increases, the system becomes more and more inefficient and unrealistic, so it only works with small crew numbers. As stated before, this system also relies on the crew’s own moral values and social responsibilities to function. Because everyone gets a voice and there are so few people, crew members have a large amount of influence over decisions that are made. Mutiny is also possible, and so additional measures must be taken to prevent this.

How does your colony choose members of the executive branch?
Tasks and responsibilities during the mission will be predetermined on Earth, and on Mars each crew member will serve as a “cabinet Minister” of their respective areas. Again, the mission commander will serve as the prime minister and will oversee the roles and responsibilities of all crew members. This system is extremely efficient, as with small groups of people there simply isn't enough manpower to organize large committees and organizations. With so many tasks to take care of and so little people to do those tasks on Mars, the best option is also the simplest- to have each person cover all responsibilities for their departments. Some cons to this however, are that there is a high risk of error in the system as only one person is in charge of each category. As such, there are less people to double check processes and decisions, and a higher risk of failure. Again, this system relies heavily on the ability of the crew, and so the crew selected will need to be extremely capable.

What role will political parties play in your colony?
Political parties will likely play little to no role in our colony as there will simply not be enough people to form any sort of party. Political parties often act as a medium between the people and the government, but in a system where the people are the government and the government are the people, this is not needed. This will make the system less complicated and more efficient, but will also need adjustments once our colony;s population increases. Would there be any government control over your media - how free is your media to report issues in your society? What are the pros and cons to this? The only likely source of media on our colony would probably be data transmissions back to Earth, in which case all issues and information must be relayed over freely without any censorship. This will ensure mission control knows as much information about the status of the colony as possible should any issues arise. However, this is a flawed system as the crew themselves control all information output to Earth and any member could simply refuse to relay information. To keep this from happening, the system relies on the morals of the crew and the threat of punishment once back on Earth from mission control.

JUSTICE

Rights & Freedoms of the United Innovator Colonists

* All Rights are subject to reasonable limitation placed by the law*


Fundamental Freedoms

  1. Expression - All ideas are accepted. Criticisms should be kept polite and constructive.
  2. Conscience & Belief - Any beliefs, as long as they still support and cooperate with the colony
  3. Association - Spend time with those they desire to

Equality Rights

  1. Everyone treated equally, regardless of age, race, gender, beliefs, or job.
  2. All jobs considered important, even if some require more knowledge than others.

Mobility Rights

  1. Mars Colonists may request to work with any group in any department as long as they meet the requirements
  2. They may request to move living quarters if they are not satisfied with their current accommodations.

Information Rights

  1. The right to information. All Mars Colonists have the right to know what’s going on, unless it interferes with privacy rights in Section 6

Legal Rights

  1. Protection against harassment, illegal search.
  2. Fair trial
  3. Innocent until proven guilty
  4. No cruel or physical punishment
  5. Crimes will only be revealed to the public if it is deemed unsafe to keep that information hidden.

How we deal with crimes

  • Rehabilitative system which includes:
    • Having the victim(if applicable) & offender work towards a solution together.
    • Focused on making amends
    • No unrelated punishment, only natural consequences
      • For example, if the crew member was caught stealing food, then they would have their food storage access privileges revoked for a certain period of time
      • However, they would not have any other privileges (such as living quarter access, security room access, etc) revoked just to ‘punish’ them.


Look at the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms - do you want to apply these same rights, or do you want to create different rights for your people.  Are there some rights you don’t want to give colonists because this is a different society and environment from what Canadians experience. You may want to take the rights that exist in Canada and adapt them somewhat to better fit the needs of your colony.

  • Since we have Unity and Equality as central goals, we should have similar rights for everyone
  • There should be very few special needs as everyone on the mission must be capable (No disabilities, backgrounds don’t matter much)

What about collective rights - why do we have them in Canada?  Are there any groups in your Mars colony that should have special rights.

  • There should not be any “special groups” 
  • Although perhaps if colonists ever started reproducing, the mothers would need some time to recoup. 
  • Are we even going to leave elderly people around in the early stages of colonization? 
  • Maybe we should send them back to Earth before they get too old to be productive, or else they will take up many more resources than necessary. Maybe even a semi dystopian society where we get rid of them…

You need to have at least 6 specific rights, AND justify the choice of these rights by explaining both the pros and cons of establishing those rights.  

  • Freedom of speech - colonists have the right to say what they want, as long as it does not defame or offend society.
    • Pro: people will be able to contribute to the decisions of the colony to make it better
    • Con: it can cause conflict, which is a concern since we are such a small group
  • Mobility rights - while citizens have the right to leave the colony, they can only do so with the government permission by applying for a visa.
    • Pro: people like having the freedom to move around and go on vacation
    • Con: with such a small colony, we may need to limit how many people are coming and going to protect the economy and prevent the spread of disease - this may frustrate people who cannot get a visa when they want.
